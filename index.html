TO FIGHT THE PROSTATE CANCER!

Prostate cancer Initiation, Metastasis and Resistance, under the direction of Dr. Yakun LUO dissect the cellular and molecular mechanisms involved in tumor progression to propose novel therapeutic targets against prostate cancer.

Welcome to Luo's group - to fight Prostate Cancer

Prostate cancer is one of the malignancies that affects men and significantly contributes to increased mortality rates in men globally. Despite advances in prostate cancer screening and treatment, available therapy options, particularly in later stages of the disease, remain limited and the treatment-resistant setting represents a serious unmet medical need. The lethality of advanced disease is driven by the lack of therapeutic regimens capable of generating durable responses in the setting of extreme tumor heterogeneity on the genetic and cell biological levels. The mainstay of therapy for men with metastatic prostate cancer is androgen deprivation therapy (ADT). Prostate cancer arises as a hormonally-driven disease, relying on activation of the androgen receptor (AR) by endogenous ligands (e.g. testosterone and dihydrotestosterone) for growth, and is initially responsive to ADT, which inhibits testosterone production by the testes. Emergence of resistance to ADT leads to castration-resistant prostate cancer (CRPC). AR signaling represents the main driver of prostate cancer progression and understanding its regulation has advanced both therapies that target this signaling axis and our knowledge of the mechanisms that underlie treatment resistance. The majority of castration resistant prostate tumors retain their dependence on the AR cistrome, primarily through genomic amplification or mutation of the AR gene and alterations in androgen synthesis, among other mechanisms. More potent next-generation AR-targeting agents now have a dominant role in the treatment of CRPC and hormone naive metastatic prostate cancer in combination with ADT. These drugs act through inhibition of extra-gonadal androgen formation (ie., abiraterone acetate) or through direct inhibition of the AR (ie., enzalutamide, apalutamide, darolutamide). Nonetheless, resistance to these potent agents inevitably occurs, and cross-resistance limits their utility when used sequentially. Further, there remains a subset of patients with rapidly progressive, virulent disease.

Projects

Initiation

Dissect the role played by menin during the tumorigenesis of prostate cells.

Metastasis

Cellular and molecular mechanisms underlying the role played by menin regulating invasion and metastasis in AR+ prostate cancer cells.

Neuroendocrine differentiation

ATOH8 alters the antiandrogen enzalutamide-induced prostate cancer neuroendocrine differentiation.

Metabolism

CirBNC2 modulates the metabolic signaling of prostate cancer cells via the AR/ACSL6 axis.
